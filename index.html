Muhammad Anas Yousaf

The Visionary: Sam Walton (1945–1962)

  • The Core Philosophy: Sam Walton purchased a Ben Franklin franchise in Newport, AR (1945). He adopted a counterintuitive strategy: Lower margins + High volume = Success.
  • Innovation Lab: Opened "Walton's 5&10" in Bentonville (1950). Introduced self-service concepts and central checkout registers to reduce labor costs.
  • The First Walmart: Opened July 2, 1962, in Rogers, Arkansas.
  • Unique Strategy: While competitors (Kmart) targeted cities, Walton targeted rural towns (pop. 5k–25k) that were underserved.

Economic Impact: "The Walmart Effect"

  • Deflationary Force: Walmart's pressure on suppliers lowers prices across the entire retail industry, not just in its own stores.

5. MIS They Use

Yaser
G1F22UBSCS193

Inventory Visibility: RFID Technology

Accuracy: RFID improved inventory accuracy from 63% to 95-99%.
  • Mandate: Suppliers in Home, Toys, Electronics, and Apparel must tag items with UHF RFID inlays.
  • Function: Handheld readers scan hundreds of items instantly (non-line-of-sight).
  • Impact: Eliminated "phantom inventory" and reduced out-of-stocks by 16%. Essential for accurate Online Pickup.
